Methodology

This dashboard presents a cross-cultural analysis of academic papers on AI ethics from the CEI Literature Vault. The analysis examines how cultural context shapes AI values discourse across dimensions.

Author Geography Classification: Each paper's first author was classified into one of 10 world regions based on institutional affiliation, extracted from paper metadata. Classification methods included:

    WEIRD Scoring: Each paper received a composite WEIRD score (0-100) based on five sub-dimensions: Western, Educated, Industrialized, Rich, and Democratic. Scores reflect the cultural context of the research institution.

    Framework Detection: Papers were scored on 34 value dimensions using keyword-based scoring (0-100 scale). A score ≥ 50 indicates substantive engagement with a framework. Frameworks include:

    • Non-Western (7):
    • Religious Traditions (14):
    • Moral Psychology (8):

    Limitations:

    • Author affiliation is a proxy for cultural context; many researchers work across cultures
    • Keyword-based scoring may miss nuanced philosophical engagement
    • The corpus has a strong Global North / WEIRD bias reflecting publication patterns
    • Temporal trends are influenced by the vault's acquisition strategy
    • N/S and E/W classifications are simplifications of complex geopolitical realities
